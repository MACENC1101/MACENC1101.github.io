Prompt

You've worked hard on your drafts, and now's the time to share them and to give your classmates some feedback on their drafts! Here, you will upload your draft of Essay 1 and complete peer reviews of at least two of your classmates' drafts of Essay 1. You will be automatically split into groups with this discussion board tool (you'll only be able to see responses from those in your group), and within those groups, you should review at least two of your classmates' essays. In order to share your work, please create a new post in this discussion board and attach whatever you have thus far for Essay 1. Whether what you have is an outline, a description of your plans, a mockup of your final composition, a more complete draft of your work, etc., is up to you, but you must submit something for your classmates to review. You will respond to each other's work by replying to each other's posts. This peer review consists of three parts:

My Response

Some concerns I have right now with my draft would be my syntax placement, and and my repetitive use of the word I and my. If anyone knows any good methods for reducing them in context that would help. (really the only part that is actually in draft form is probably the conclusion section but I ask for leniency there) (Title: Literacy in Coding)

(From Fabiana Granados) Hi Matthew, I really enjoyed reading your essay since I have some coding experience in Python. Although you don't necessarily talk about reading and writing, your examples of coding teaching you literacy are still effective. About your syntax concern, I think most of the issues come from the length of your sentences, and having multiple ideas within one sentence. I think breaking down longer complex sentences into shorter simpler ones could make sure the essay flow is clear. For example, for the sentence, "At first, I still struggled with writing classes and objects; then attempting to learn how to effectively use public and private variables and dealing with some of Java’s less appealing syntax when trying to use and implement methods on objects." you could remove some of the ands and create 2 sentences instead. To help with the repetitive use of I and my, you could start the sentence with the actual action or idea instead of with yourself. I enjoyed the main idea of your essay and how you creatively compared coding to reading and writing.
