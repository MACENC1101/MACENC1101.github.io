Assignment 2

DISCUSS: Reading Response #2

Prompt

After reading Laura Bolin Carroll, “Backpacks vs. Briefcases: Steps Toward Rhetorical Analysis,” think about some times when you have been the rhetor responsible for creating rhetoric to address a particular rhetorical situation, then focus on one recent example and answer the following questions in roughly 250 words total: What is a rhetorical situation you have found yourself in as the rhetor (the person creating the rhetoric/writing) recently? What was the exigence in this situation? Why did you feel the need to create some rhetoric to address it? Who was your audience? How did this affect the choices you made? What constraints limited your choices as the rhetor? What could you learn about yourself from examining more of your communication choices through this lens?

My Response

I created a guide in the form of a website, it was for a Mega Man game created in 2006 and re-released recently; which in this case counts as a rhetorical situation. It is based off forums which have an incredible amount of useful information, but they can not necessarily be considered a guide as it requires a lot of searching and wasted time. With all this information I saw the need to compile it since there were other players that were going to fall into the same problem of searching too. The audience is more than likely a group of people in their twenties to thirties who do not particularly have the time to replay the entire game. Considering my audience, I choose to create a basic HTML site with a table of contents and a massive list of all the components needed to 100% it without the lost time of searching forums. My constraints in this scenario were the fact that I did not want to pay for a website domain, and using a google document felt unprofessional, this led to me considering GitHub Pages which allowed me to have a website with the small loss of not being able to change my domain name immediately. Something I learned about myself is that I may over-explain myself when writing, as it is something I have picked up over time. This might be due to one of my bad habits, as when I speak it tends to come off very quiet; this more often leads to creating longer explanations for responses rather actually attempting to raise my voice which translates here.

(From Sofia Jaramillo Chavez) Writing an appeal for a UCF parking violation is a recent rhetorical situation that I have encountered. I was the rhetor as I wrote to explain the circumstances surrounding the parking ticket and to persuade a reviewer to dismiss the citation. The exigence was the citation itself. I saw the need to write an appeal as I believed there were circumstances that could justify or explain why I parked where I did. I hoped to convey a sense if understanding of the situation to the reviewer so they may consider dismissing the citation. As I knew that UCF parking services staff would ultimately review my appeal, knowing who would read my message directly affected how I communicated it. Therefore, I attempted to write respectfully, professionally, and clearly. I also made sure to going straight to the point by providing all necessary information rather than using unnecessary language or tone that could exprés frustration. Several constraints also limited my options. The first constraint was that the space to describe my circumstances was limited. Secondly, I needed to maintain professionalism while arguing my position. Lastly, I needed to provide enough evidence to support my appeal. When viewed from a rhetorical lens, this experience showed how often we adjust our communication to our audience and purpose. It also illustrated the importance of selecting an appropriate tone when communicating the most relevant information when attempting to convince another individual.

(Response to Sofia Jaramillo Chavez) Hey Sofia, I think this is a great example of a rhetorical situation, more so because it differs from my situation that did not have a need but rather a wanting to create a rhetoric. It would force someone to think constructively about the method of communication, and how to write professionally for someone that may not be concerned about your message and situation. My scenario involved people who were searching for a rhetoric, which created a different relationship between the author and audience as more control is given to the author. This showed me how constraint differences can be massive depending on a rhetoric chosen.
